Parmigiana

Description

The queen of single-course meals, the lifter of spirits: eggplant parmigiana. It’s a recipe shared by regions in both the north and south of Italy.

The name Parmigiana is said to come from the Sicilian Parmiciana, which in dialect refers to the blinds made up of wooden slats stacked on one another.

Ingredients

  • Eggplants: 1.76 lb (800 g)
  • Mozzarella Cheese: 8.8 oz (250 g)
  • Grated Parmesan Cheese: 2.8 oz (80 g)
  • Basil: 1 tuft
  • Peeled Tomatoes Whipped, or Tomato Puree: 26.5 oz (750 g)
  • Extra-Virgin Olive Oil (EVO): 3 tablespoons
  • Sunflower Seed Oil to fry: 50.7 fl oz (1.5 l)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. First wash and cut by the long side the eggplants in regular slices, then put them to drain in a colander after having sprinkled every layer of coarse salt. Leave it for about an hour with a weight on it (for ex. put a dish with a pot full of water on it).
  2. Squeeze and dry well the eggplant slices with a clean rag, then fry them in plenty of hot seed oil. Once well browned, drain them from the oil and put aside to dry from the exceeding oil.
  3. Prepare, now, the tomato sauce: heat a few quantity of extra virgin olive oil in a pan (cover the whole bottom), add two peeled garlic cloves and wait until the oil begins to sizzle. Only then add the peeled tomatoes.
  4. Season with a tuft of basil and salt. Cook on a moderate heat until the sauce is pretty thick.
  5. Now that you have all the ingredients ready you just have to assemble the parmigiana: put some tomato sauce on the bottom of an oven dish, then make the first layer of eggplants and cover with the mozzarella cut into slices, parmesan and again some tomato sauce. Continue in this order until the ingredients are used up.
  6. Top the parmigiana with a layer of eggplants covered with the sauce and grated Parmesan.
  7. Bake it and let cook for 25 minutes at 180 ° C.
  8. Once cooked, let the parmigiana rest for about a couple of hours, it will certainly be better!
